Explain a situation where you have had an ethical decision to make. Discuss how you weighed up the values involved in that decision, the decision you made and the consequences of the action you took.

I honestly can't think of a serious ethical decision I've had to make recently. But rather a set of small ones. I've been working in my parents restaurant/cinema over the winter and the busiest days it gets really, really busy. Sometimes when I'd dropped the ball in some way or another - forgetting to put a customer's order through, forgetting to clean a cinema - I'd instinctively tell white lies to cover up for myself. After doing this a few times, I noticed what I was doing and was quite disappointed with myself. I'd always thought I was a radically honest person. but under stress, and when being held accountable by my workmates, I had allowed my standards to slip. since that, and especially since dwelling on this idea while doing this exercise, I've committed to being that same radically honest person I have been in the past


Describe how your culture has influenced your values and identity.

My family culture is very heavily focussed on taking responsibility. People who complain or seek handouts form others are not looked upon kindly in my family. My Mum has always told me that it's my responsibility to fix my own problems. As a kid, I found that confusing as I thought that most of my problems were other people's fault. But, growing up, I began to understand what she'd meant. Even if the problems weren't my fault, no one else was going to fix them for me. They may not have been my fault (however, many times, they were) but they were always my responsibility and blaming others was not going to change that in the slightest.

Growing up with very encouraging, self employed parents, I always had a high expectation for what life could be for me. In my family culture, success was always praised and never demonized as it often is in pop-culture. A 'normal' job with an average salary was never even a consideration for me. while others in my year group may have changed their dream job from astronaut to something more realistic, my goals haven't shrunk.

Kindness was also a hugely important value in my upbringing. My Mum always expected me to be the kid who included the uncool kids. She made a big deal of the seniors who took me under their wing and the kids who included me in games so that I could learn how special that can be. She constantly reminded me that my granddad 'never made a joke at someone else's expense' and although that may be a bit of a strict rule to follow (who doesn't like a bit of teasing with friends) the point still stands.
