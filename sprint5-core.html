Final Reflections

Sprint 5 Core
Friday October 2

What are three new things you have learned about yourself and your ego as a result of the core learning?

  1. I haven't been being as honest as I would like recently
  2. I find it hard to take breaks when I'm on a roll
  3. I often try to solve a problem with brute force when a more methodical approach would work better

What are the roles of empathy and self-awareness in learning and programming?

A lot of the time it's easy to get into an intellectual rut while programming and get lost in your own head. Self-awareness allows you to mediate your attention and approach a problem in an effective and efficient way.


Empathy allows us to connect with our fellow developers and work together productively. The productivity of two people working together is often greater than the sum of its parts.


What has surprised you the most about the core learning?

Honestly, I thought it would be mostly useless and a waste of time. Although I still haven't loved it, there were some moments where I could see the value in what I was doing. For example, the values and identity activity caused a surprising amount of introspection.


What were the most challenging aspects of the core learning?

I found the lack of a clear structure to be very annoying. I like clear, logical instructions and lots of the core material was not like this at all.


Why do you think we, a programming school, are spending so much time focusing on core learning in a web development bootcamp course?

Because the kind of person who takes a programming course - like me - is typically a highly logical, unemotional person and it's important to develop both aspects of the personality.
